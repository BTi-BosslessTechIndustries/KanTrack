# KanTrack Roadmap Philosophy
## How to Build Without Losing the Soul

---

## 1. The Core Principle
KanTrack builds **clarity multipliers**, not feature lists.

**Roadmap question:**  
👉 Will this make users feel more organized, calm, and in control?

If not, it is noise.

---

## 2. Build Depth Before Breadth
Grow downward (deeper usefulness), not sideways (more surface area).  
Make the kanban more powerful for personal thinking, not more crowded.

---

## 3. Solve Real Friction, Not Hypothetical Needs
Roadmap inputs come from:
- Observed user behavior
- Repeated pain points
- Your own daily usage

Not from:
- Competitor parity
- Industry expectations
- Hypothetical scenarios

---

## 4. Clarity > Capability
Prefer features that reduce confusion over those that add power.

KanTrack optimizes for **mental ease**, not technical ability.

---

## 5. The Anti‑Bloat Rule
A feature must justify its permanent presence.

Accept only if it:
- Simplifies planning
- Reduces steps
- Removes friction

Reject if it adds menus, toggles, or settings without removing pain.

---

## 6. Personal Workflow First
Priority order:
1. Core personal workflow (planning, movement, visibility)
2. Context (notes, knowledge)
3. Quality of life (daily smoothing)
4. Expansion (only if it strengthens 1–3)

Anything else waits.

---

## 7. No Features for Hypothetical Teams
Do not prioritize:
- Reporting
- Oversight
- Manager visibility
- Coordination dashboards

Individual usefulness always comes first.

---

## 8. The Daily Use Test
👉 Will someone use this every day?

If rarely or situationally, defer.

---

## 9. Ship When It Feels Inevitable
The best features feel obvious once released.

If it requires explanation to justify itself, it is not ready.

---

## 10. Protect the Aha Moment
Never compromise fast onboarding or instant usefulness.  
Preserve the “feeling organized” moment.

---

## 11. Build for the User You Were Yesterday
Your own workflow is a valid signal when aligned with the doctrine.

---

## 12. Delay Everything That Can Wait
Let time filter ideas.  
If it still matters months later, it may be real.

---

## 13. Resist Feature Parity Gravity
Do not chase competitors’ breadth.  
Preserve intentional restraint.

---

## 14. Growth Through Trust
Consistency builds trust.  
Avoid sudden directional changes.

---

## 15. The Identity Test
👉 Does this make KanTrack more like itself, or less?

If less — reject.

---

## 16. The Long Game
KanTrack builds a category, not a race.  
Alignment beats speed.

---

## 17. When in Doubt, Wait
If clarity is missing, pause.  
A restrained roadmap is stronger than a noisy one.

---

## End of Roadmap Philosophy
