# KanTrack Decision Checklist

Use this checklist whenever evaluating a feature, change, or opportunity.

---

## Core Alignment

**Does this increase personal workflow clarity?**  
If not, it likely does not belong.

**Does this strengthen user autonomy?**  
KanTrack exists to restore control, not remove it.

**Does this reduce cognitive load?**  
If it adds complexity, reconsider.

---

## Individual‑First Test

**Is this primarily for the individual user?**  
If it benefits managers or observers more than the user, reject it.

**Would a self‑directed professional find this valuable without being told to use it?**

---

## Privacy & Ownership

**Does this preserve user data ownership?**

**Does it avoid surveillance, tracking, or performance measurement?**

**Can users still export their data freely afterward?**

---

## Simplicity & Focus

**Does this keep the product focused on personal workflow?**

**Does it maintain the kanban as the central thinking surface?**

**Is this solving a real problem or just adding capability?**

---

## Anti‑Drift Safeguard

**Could this push KanTrack toward becoming a team management tool?**

**Would this make the product feel like enterprise software?**

**Does it risk turning the app into a trap or dependency?**

If yes to any, stop and reconsider.

---

## Long‑Term Sustainability

**Can this be maintained without compromising independence?**

**Will it still make sense in five years?**

**Does it align with the doctrine, not just current demand?**

---

## Final Question

> **Does this help someone feel more organized, calm, and in control?**

If the answer is clearly yes, it belongs in KanTrack.

---

## End of Checklist
