# KanTrack Homepage Structure Blueprint
## A Step‑by‑Step Layout for the Public Homepage

This blueprint defines the **order, purpose, and message** of each section on the KanTrack homepage.

Goal:
Guide a visitor from curiosity → recognition → trust → action.

---

## 1. HERO — Immediate Identity

### Purpose
Instantly communicate:

- What KanTrack is
- Who it is for
- Why it is different

### Content

**Headline**
Organize Your Work on Your Terms

**Subheadline**
A private workspace for individuals — no accounts, no tracking, no noise.

**Primary Call to Action**
Start Instantly

**Secondary Line**
Works offline. Your data stays on your device.

### Notes
Must be visible without scrolling.

---

## 2. BUILT FOR THE PERSON DOING THE WORK

### Purpose
Make the right audience feel understood.

### Message
Contrast KanTrack with team‑centric tools.

Example points:

- Team tools coordinate work
- KanTrack helps you manage your day
- Your personal plan matters

---

## 3. PRIVACY & CONTROL

### Purpose
Remove skepticism and build trust.

### Key Messages

- Local‑first by default
- No surveillance
- No behavior tracking
- Export anytime

Focus on ownership and independence.

---

## 4. WHAT IT FEELS LIKE TO USE

### Purpose
Create emotional connection.

### Emphasize Outcomes

- Feeling organized
- Calm planning
- Clear priorities
- Reduced overwhelm

Avoid feature lists.

---

## 5. FITS INTO YOUR EXISTING WORKFLOW

### Purpose
Show practicality.

### Message

KanTrack complements required tools.

Examples:

- Use it alongside Jira
- Personal layer above corporate systems
- No disruption to existing workflows

---

## 6. AN APP, NOT A TRAP

### Purpose
Differentiate from SaaS dependency.

### Key Points

- No lock‑in
- No forced accounts
- Your data is yours
- Leave anytime

Reinforces trust.

---

## 7. FINAL CALL TO ACTION

### Purpose
Convert interest into use.

### Message

Start organizing your work today.

Reinforce:

- No signup required
- Works immediately
- Free local mode

CTA should be visually prominent.

---

## Layout Principles

- Clean spacing
- Minimal visual noise
- Clear reading flow
- Calm color palette

The page should feel like the product:
clear, calm, and focused.

---

## End of Blueprint
