The problem

Work tools were built for organizations. Not for you.

Jira tells your company what is happening. Notion stores your team's knowledge. Asana tracks your sprint against goals set by someone else. None of them were built to answer the question you ask every morning.

"What should I do right now?"
🗂️

Too many dashboards

Tasks scattered across Jira, Slack, email, and sticky notes. No single clear view.

👁️

Tools that watch you

Enterprise software measures your output and reports it upward. You are the subject, not the user.

🔒

Your data is hostage

Vendor lock-in, sudden price hikes, account requirements. Your work is trapped in someone else's system.

Most professionals already maintain a hidden layer of personal systems — a private notebook, a text file, sticky notes — just to stay sane. KanTrack formalizes that private layer into a purpose-built tool that is entirely yours.
What KanTrack is

Your personal command center for work.

A calm, focused planning surface that helps you see everything you are working on in one place. Four columns: To Do, In Progress, On Hold, and Done. Move tasks as work progresses. Attach the context that matters.

Feature highlight

Private by Design

This is not a feature. It is the foundation everything else is built on.

Local-first storage
Everything lives in your browser's IndexedDB and localStorage. Nothing is transmitted, synced, or logged anywhere.
Fully offline
No CDN dependencies at runtime. Every asset is bundled. Works without an internet connection.
No analytics. No telemetry. No cookies.
Not ever. Not in a future update. This is a design rule, not a policy.
Browser-enforced CSP
A strict Content Security Policy blocks all third-party scripts and network connections at the browser level.
Storage monitoring
Calm notice at 70% quota, backup prompt at 85%. Requests durable storage permission to protect your data.
Privacy is the default state
You do not configure it. You do not opt in. It is simply how the application works.

Why it matters

What Sets KanTrack Apart

The local-first software movement is a direct response to the structural problems of centralized cloud tools — breaches, lock-in, surveillance by default. KanTrack sits at that intersection.

If Jira tracks projects, KanTrack tracks your day.
Privacy enforced at the browser level Individual-first not organization-first Local-first architecture No vendor lock-in Open source
Complements your existing tools
KanTrack does not replace Jira, Notion, or Asana. Those tools answer organizational questions. KanTrack answers the individual question they were never designed to address.
Built on principles that do not drift
Every engineering decision is governed by public philosophy documents. Growth pressure, investor expectations, and feature creep cannot override them.
The road ahead: an optional encrypted vault for cross-device backup and sync, and a future desktop mode for unlimited local storage. Paid tiers will extend capability without removing freedom. That is a design rule, not a marketing position.
